# Day-of Checklist

Public-safe execution plan for Kate's 8/1 birthday party. Theme: **dress for the wrong event** — guests come dressed for the party they wish they were attending.

## Guest Count Assumption

- Plan core supplies for **15 confirmed guests**.
- Stage flexible extras for **up to 25 guests**.
- Keep all setup modular so stations can expand if more people are invited.

## Setup Timeline

### Day Before

- Confirm final guest count range and note any new dietary constraints.
- Charge speaker, backup speaker, camera/phone battery pack, and any lights.
- Build the party playlist and a shorter "arrival energy" queue.
- Chill drinks that are already on hand.
- Put trash bags, paper towels, wipes, tape, scissors, markers, and serving tools in one supplies bin.
- Pre-label any activity materials that need instructions.
- Clear surfaces for food, drinks, activities, and photo props.

### Morning Of

- Do a full clutter reset of guest-facing spaces.
- Put backup toilet paper, hand soap, hand towels, and a plunger where the host can access them fast.
- Set out non-perishable snacks and serving pieces without opening anything stale-prone.
- Put empty coolers, ice buckets, or drink bins in position.
- Create a hidden-but-accessible restock zone for snacks, cups, napkins, paper towels, trash bags, and ice.
- Confirm playlist device, speaker pairing, and volume.

### 4 Hours Before Guests

- Start any food that needs long prep or warming.
- Place signage or labels for food, drinks, activities, and trash/recycling.
- Set up the photo moment with props, good lighting, and enough room for group shots.
- Stage the first activity so it is obvious without requiring a speech.
- Put out trash and recycling containers in visible spots.
- Create a small host command station: guest list, marker, tape, scissors, lighter, stain remover, pain reliever, safety pins, and phone charger.

### 2 Hours Before Guests

- Put drinks on ice.
- Plate or bowl shelf-stable snacks.
- Set out cups, napkins, plates, utensils, and serving utensils.
- Queue music at a lively but conversation-friendly volume.
- Do one full guest-path walkthrough: entrance flow, food access, drink access, seating, activity space, bathroom access, trash access.
- Move anything fragile, personal, or easy to trip over out of guest flow.

### 30 Minutes Before Guests

- Put out final fresh food.
- Light candles or turn on ambient lighting if using them.
- Take quick photos of the finished setup before guests arrive.
- Start the arrival playlist.
- Switch phone to charged/available mode.
- Fill a water station.
- Take 5 minutes to reset yourself: eat a snack, drink water, and put on the wrong-event outfit.

### First Arrival Wave

- Greet each guest with high energy and point them to:
  - Drinks
  - Food
  - Bathroom
  - Photo moment
  - Activity
- Capture or encourage early outfit photos before people set down props or layers.
- Give guests permission to place brought drinks in the drink zone.
- Keep the first 20 minutes low-friction: no complicated games until the room has warmed up.

### Peak Party

- Refresh food and drink stations every 30-45 minutes.
- Start one planned activity when there are enough guests for momentum.
- Prompt photos when the best outfits or funniest mismatches are present.
- Keep music slightly more energetic after the room fills.
- Quietly remove empty containers, stray trash, and abandoned plates during natural transitions.

### Final Hour

- Bring out any remaining snacks or late-night food.
- Take final group or themed photos.
- Lower the energy gradually with a less intense playlist.
- Put obvious trash/recycling containers near exit flow.
- Offer guests a simple way to take leftovers or drinks they brought.

## Zones and Stations

### Welcome / Drop Zone

- Clear landing spot for bags, jackets, costume props, and drink contributions.
- Optional sign: "Drop props here, grab a drink, get photographed."
- Keep the path clear so arrivals do not bottleneck.

### Food Zone

- Main snacks and food on one surface with plates, napkins, utensils, and serving tools at the start of the flow.
- Put allergen or dietary notes directly beside relevant items.
- Keep backup food in the restock zone, not on the table all at once.

### Drink Zone

- Guest-provided drinks go here.
- Include cups, bottle opener, corkscrew, ice, water, and non-alcoholic options.
- Keep towels or wipes nearby for spills.
- Put recycling/trash within arm's reach.

### Photo / Costume Zone

- The selected 8x10 Walgreens backdrop, securely hung and fully lit.
- Props: event signs, fake wristbands, name tags, medals, bouquet, conference badge, sports pennant, birthday sash, or anything that reinforces "wrong event."
- Use this zone early before outfits get rumpled.

### Activity Zone

- Choose a place where people can participate casually without blocking food or drinks.
- Keep instructions visible and short.
- Avoid activities that require every guest to stop talking at once.

### Chill / Conversation Zone

- Seating or standing room away from the loudest speaker.
- Water and a small snack nearby if possible.
- Useful for guests who do not know many people yet.

### Supplies / Restock Zone

- Out of the main party flow but easy for the host to reach.
- Store backup cups, napkins, plates, utensils, paper towels, trash bags, wipes, first-aid basics, tape, marker, scissors, extra batteries, and chargers.

## Host Duties

### Primary Host Loop

Every 30-45 minutes, scan:

1. Are guests welcomed and connected?
2. Is food still appetizing?
3. Are drinks cold and easy to find?
4. Are trash/recycling containers obvious and not overflowing?
5. Is the music matching the room?
6. Has a photo/activity moment happened recently?
7. Does anything need to be removed before it becomes annoying?

### Delegatable Jobs

Assign these to trusted guests if needed:

- Drink restock captain
- Photo prompt captain
- Trash/recycling sweep
- Playlist helper
- Activity explainer
- Late-arrival greeter

### Aggressive Hosting Prompts

- "You look incredible. Photo first, drink second."
- "Tell me what event you dressed for."
- "You two both look like you're at completely different disasters; please meet."
- "Everyone with props, photo zone in two minutes."
- "If you brought drinks, they go in the drink zone and you get immediate hero status."

## Supplies Staging

### Put Out

- Plates, cups, napkins, utensils
- Serving bowls and trays
- Serving spoons/tongs
- Bottle opener and corkscrew
- Ice buckets, coolers, or drink bins
- Water station
- Trash and recycling bags
- Paper towels and wipes
- Food labels and marker
- Speaker and charger

### Keep in Restock

- Extra cups, plates, napkins, utensils
- Backup trash bags
- Extra paper towels and wipes
- First-aid basics
- Safety pins or fashion tape
- Phone charger and battery pack
- Extra ice if available
- Shelf-stable backup snacks

## Food and Drink Staging

- Put the most durable snacks out first.
- Hold anything melty, soggy, or hot-sensitive until closer to arrivals.
- Keep food quantities in waves so the table looks full without exposing everything at once.
- Place water where guests do not need to ask for it.
- Put non-alcoholic options in the same zone as other drinks, not as an afterthought.
- Keep brought drinks consolidated so they do not scatter across the party.
- For 15 guests: one main food surface is likely enough.
- For 20-25 guests: split snacks and drinks into separate surfaces to reduce crowding.

## Activity Staging

### Easy Theme Activities

- **Wrong-Event Name Tags:** guests write "I'm here for the __________ event."
- **Walgreens Photo Station:** take individual, pair, and group photos throughout the night.
- **Easter Egg Hunt:** raffle tickets inside the eggs feed the final prize drawing.
- **St. Patrick's Day Bean Bag Toss:** use the existing banner as a drop-in carnival station.
- **Egg Drop Contest:** team build, presentation, and safe drop.
- **New Year's Countdown and Kiss:** run at the midpoint of the actual party.

### Activity Timing

- Start with name tags and passive Walgreens photo prompts during arrivals.
- Do the egg hunt once at least half the expected guests have arrived.
- Run the bean bag toss as a drop-in station while people mingle.
- Schedule the egg drop before the midpoint countdown.
- Save the raffle drawing and final group photo for the last hour.

## Music and Photo Moments

### Music

- Arrival: upbeat, familiar, conversation-friendly.
- Peak: more energetic and playful.
- Activity moments: lower volume before giving instructions.
- Final hour: slightly calmer, nostalgic, or singalong-friendly.

### Photo Moments

- Before guests arrive: empty setup photos.
- First arrival wave: individual outfit photos.
- Peak party: themed pairs and group photos.
- Before anyone leaves early: full group attempt if possible.
- Final hour: funniest remaining outfit/prop photos.

## Cleanup Plan

### During Party

- Do small resets every 30-45 minutes.
- Replace trash/recycling before containers overflow.
- Consolidate empty cans, bottles, and cups.
- Wipe sticky drink spots immediately.
- Pull food that no longer looks good.

### End of Night

- Collect trash and recycling.
- Put away reusable serving pieces.
- Consolidate leftovers.
- Check for guest belongings and costume props.
- Do a final floor/table/surface pass.
- Start one load or soak of anything that will be worse tomorrow.

### Next Morning

- Finish dishes and surfaces.
- Return borrowed items.
- Send photos or thank-yous.
- Note what worked for future hosting.

## Contingency Notes

### If More Than 15 Guests Come

- Move to wave-based food restocking.
- Split food and drinks into separate zones.
- Ask one guest to own photos and one guest to watch drink restock.
- Put out extra trash/recycling containers early.

### If Guests Arrive Slowly

- Keep music upbeat but not too loud.
- Prompt early arrivals into photos and low-pressure theme explanations.
- Delay structured activities until the room has enough people.

### If Everyone Arrives at Once

- Host greeting should be fast: welcome, drink zone, food zone, photo zone.
- Skip long explanations.
- Start a passive activity immediately so guests have something to do while flow settles.

### If Food Runs Low

- Bring out backup snacks.
- Re-plate remaining food to look intentional.
- Push drinks, water, and activities while food resets.

### If Drinks Run Low

- Highlight water and non-alcoholic options.
- Consolidate remaining drinks in one visible place.
- Ask a trusted guest if they can coordinate a quick restock without making it a public issue.

### If Energy Drops

- Change the playlist.
- Call for a photo category.
- Start a 5-minute wrong-event toast or awards moment.
- Introduce two guests through their costumes.

### If Something Spills or Breaks

- Clear people away first.
- Use the supplies bin for wipes, towels, trash bags, or tape.
- Reset the area quickly and move the party flow elsewhere if needed.

### If the Guest Count Hits 25

- Prioritize flow over decoration.
- Keep paths open.
- Restock in smaller, more frequent waves.
- Use delegated jobs.
- Avoid any activity that requires everyone to sit or gather tightly.
