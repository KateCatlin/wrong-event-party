# Decor + Awards Plan

Public-safe planning notes for a 15-25 guest “dress for the wrong event” birthday party. Goal: make the theme immediately legible, funny, and a little over-hosted without needing expensive rentals or complicated setup.

## Core Joke

Everyone arrived at the wrong party on purpose. The room should feel like multiple events collided:

- New Year’s Eve countdown energy
- Baby shower softness
- Graduation ceremony formality
- Office holiday party awkwardness
- Wedding reception sentimentality
- Sports tailgate chaos
- Funeral/retirement banquet seriousness
- Bachelorette/bachelor party sparkle
- Kids’ birthday party sugar rush
- Awards show self-importance

Use clashing signs, mismatched color palettes, and overly specific labels so guests understand the bit even before everyone arrives.

## Setup Zones

### 1. Welcome Table: “Check In for the Wrong Event”

Supplies:

- Clipboard or sign-in sheet
- Name tags
- Sharpies
- Small bowl for ballots
- Printed award ballot stack
- “Wrong Event Registry” card box or gift bag

Details:

- Ask guests to write a name tag that matches their costume, not necessarily their real name.
- Examples: “Maid of Honor,” “Keynote Speaker,” “Plus One,” “Coach,” “Bride’s Cousin,” “Class of 2009,” “Undercover Caterer.”
- Put out a sign: “Welcome! If this is not the event you meant to attend, you are in the right place.”

### 2. Snack Table: “Reception / Concession / Banquet”

Make the food area look like it cannot decide what event it serves.

Signs:

- “Wedding Reception Buffet”
- “Halftime Snacks”
- “Corporate Retreat Refreshments”
- “After-Funeral Sandwiches”
- “Holiday Potluck, But Make It August”
- “Please Enjoy the Mandatory Celebration Food”

Low-effort touches:

- Trophy or ribbon next to chips
- Tiny graduation cap cutout on a dip bowl
- Birthday candles in non-cake snacks
- Place cards with dramatic names: “Ceremonial Pretzels,” “Executive Cheese,” “Pageant Veggies,” “Champagne of Sodas”

### 3. Drink Drop Zone: “Open Bar / Hydration Station / BYOB Desk”

Since guests are encouraged to bring drinks, make the drop zone part of the joke.

Signs:

- “Open Bar, Depending on What You Brought”
- “Beverage Registry”
- “Tailgate Contribution Area”
- “Please Check Your Potion, Champagne, Sports Drink, or Juice Box Here”

Add:

- Painter’s tape and marker for labeling drinks
- Ice bucket or cooler if available
- A “Best Supporting Beverage” mini-award label

### 4. Photo Moment: “Wrong Event Step-and-Repeat”

Create one obvious photo wall where people can capture costumes.

Backdrop options:

- Metallic streamers plus one classy banner
- Wrapping paper or butcher paper with repeated phrases
- A random mix of balloons, crepe paper, and one formal-looking sign
- A fake red carpet made from red wrapping paper, felt, or a red tablecloth

Backdrop phrases:

- “Wrong Event Awards”
- “Kate’s Birthday, Apparently”
- “I Thought This Was a Wedding”
- “Please Direct All Questions to the Host Committee”
- “Sponsored by Miscommunication”

Props:

- Trophy
- Bouquet
- Graduation cap
- Santa hat
- Party horns
- Clipboard
- “Reserved” sign
- Fake microphone
- Number cards for judging
- Empty frame labeled “Official Event Photo”

Photo prompt signs:

- “Pose Like You Just Realized This Is Not Your Event”
- “Take One Serious Prom Photo”
- “Take One Corporate Headshot”
- “Take One Family Holiday Card”
- “Take One Awards Acceptance Photo”

## Awards Program

Keep awards silly, fast, and generous. For 15-25 guests, aim for 8-12 awards so the ceremony feels substantial but not endless.

### Recommended Award Categories

1. **Most Convincingly at the Wrong Event**  
   For the outfit that looks most plausible, just not here.

2. **Best Commitment to the Bit**  
   For someone who stays in character, brings props, or explains their fake event beautifully.

3. **Most Overdressed for the Occasion**  
   Formalwear, pageant energy, black-tie drama, prom, gala, opera, etc.

4. **Most Underdressed for the Occasion**  
   Gym class, beach day, sick day, pajama party, little league practice, camping trip.

5. **Best Holiday Confusion**  
   Santa, Halloween, Valentine’s Day, Easter, New Year’s, Thanksgiving, patriotic holiday, etc.

6. **Best Life Milestone Energy**  
   Wedding, graduation, retirement, baby shower, divorce party, bar/bat mitzvah, first day of school.

7. **Best Corporate Event Attendee**  
   Conference badge, business casual trauma, keynote speaker, networking menace, HR-approved fun.

8. **Most Likely to Have Opened the Wrong Calendar Invite**  
   For the guest whose look screams “I followed the instructions exactly and still ended up here.”

9. **Best Prop**  
   Bouquet, clipboard, trophy, foam finger, gift bag, sash, PowerPoint clicker, fake itinerary.

10. **Best Group Costume / Accidental Table Assignment**  
   For pairs or clusters who unintentionally or intentionally look like they came from the same wrong event.

11. **Best Dramatic Entrance**  
   For whoever arrives like they are late to vows, kickoff, a keynote, a court date, or a final rose ceremony.

12. **Guest of Honor’s Choice: Most Wrong, Most Right**  
   Host pick for the person who best captures the entire theme.

### Optional Lightning Awards

Use these if people are very committed or if voting is tied:

- Best Fake Backstory
- Best Use of Seasonal Decor
- Best “I Have Somewhere Better to Be” Energy
- Most Likely to Give a Toast Unprompted
- Most Likely to Be in the Wrong Group Chat
- Best Outfit That Raises More Questions Than Answers
- Best Commitment While Still Being Comfortable

## Ballots

### Ballot Format

Print half-sheets so voting feels easy. Guests can vote for as many categories as they want.

```text
WRONG EVENT AWARDS BALLOT

Your name, if you want credit for your opinions: __________________

Most Convincingly at the Wrong Event: _____________________________
Best Commitment to the Bit: ______________________________________
Most Overdressed for the Occasion: _______________________________
Most Underdressed for the Occasion: ______________________________
Best Holiday Confusion: __________________________________________
Best Life Milestone Energy: ______________________________________
Best Corporate Event Attendee: ___________________________________
Most Likely to Have Opened the Wrong Calendar Invite: _____________
Best Prop: _______________________________________________________
Best Group Costume / Accidental Table Assignment: _________________
Best Dramatic Entrance: __________________________________________

Optional write-in award:
Category: _______________________________________________________
Winner: _________________________________________________________
```

### Voting Flow

1. Put ballots out at arrival.
2. Announce voting about 45-60 minutes in, once most people have arrived.
3. Give guests 10-15 minutes to vote.
4. Have one host/helper count while the party continues.
5. Host reserves one award as “Guest of Honor’s Choice” to avoid ties or honor someone who gets overlooked.
6. Ceremony should last 10 minutes max.

### Tie-Breakers

- Host decides based on funniest explanation.
- Applause vote.
- Sudden-death question: “What event did you think this was, and why are you late?”
- Award both people if prizes are cheap.

## Host Scripts

### Arrival Greeting

> Welcome! You are either dressed correctly or incorrectly, and both are correct. Please make a name tag, drop drinks if you brought them, and prepare your explanation for what event you thought this was.

### Theme Explanation

> Tonight’s theme is “dress for the wrong event.” The goal is not to match each other. The goal is to look like you are fully prepared for a completely different party and are making the best of it.

### Voting Announcement

> Attention guests, attendees, athletes, bridesmaids, keynote speakers, mourners, graduates, and seasonal mascots: ballots are open. Please vote based on commitment, confusion, and emotional truth.

### Awards Ceremony Opener

> Thank you all for attending the wrong event. We know you had many other obligations tonight: weddings, conferences, championship games, holiday dinners, and apparently at least one baby shower. It is time to honor excellence in being incorrectly prepared.

### Award Presentation Template

> The award for [CATEGORY] goes to [NAME], who arrived dressed for [FAKE EVENT] and made us all believe there was a separate, more specific party happening nearby.

### Acceptance Speech Prompt

> Your speech may be up to 20 seconds and should include: what event you thought this was, who invited you, and why you stayed.

### Ceremony Closer

> Congratulations to all nominees, winners, and people who still do not know what event they are at. Please continue celebrating incorrectly.

## Little Aggressive Host Details

Pick a few, not all, unless the goal is maximum chaos.

- Give each guest a “program” that lists fake agenda items:
  - 7:00 Networking / Cocktail Hour / Warmups
  - 7:45 Emotional Toast Nobody Requested
  - 8:15 Wrong Event Awards
  - 8:30 First Dance / Halftime / Q&A
  - 9:00 Cake Adjacent Moment
- Put one formal place card on a very casual snack.
- Have a “lost and found” basket containing fake items: veil, foam finger, conference badge, tiny stocking, graduation tassel.
- Play one song from wildly different event genres every so often.
- Ask guests, “Are you with the bride, the groom, the company, or the team?”
- Create a fake seating chart with categories instead of names:
  - “People Who Thought This Was Formal”
  - “People Who Thought This Was Outdoors”
  - “People Who Thought This Was a Holiday”
  - “People Who Thought There Would Be Assigned Seating”
- Put “reserved” signs on random chairs and let people ignore them.
- Make a fake “sponsor board”: “This evening made possible by unclear communication, calendar invites, and confidence.”
- Keep a clipboard and occasionally announce “We are running 11 minutes behind schedule” with no explanation.
- Hand someone a bouquet and ask them to “hold this until the ceremony.”
- Put a single Christmas ornament, graduation tassel, or tiny pumpkin somewhere unexpectedly formal.

## Day-Of Timeline

### Before Guests Arrive

- Set welcome table with name tags, ballots, pens, and ballot bowl.
- Put signs in the main gathering areas.
- Build photo wall and test one photo.
- Label snacks and drink drop zone.
- Set prizes somewhere visible but not in the way.
- Pick one person who can help count ballots.

### First 30 Minutes

- Greet people in character.
- Encourage costume explanations.
- Point guests toward name tags and ballots.
- Take photos while outfits are fresh.

### 45-60 Minutes In

- Announce that voting is open.
- Remind guests they can vote based on outfit, prop, backstory, or vibes.

### 60-75 Minutes In

- Count ballots.
- Resolve ties.
- Arrange prizes in award order.

### Awards

- Keep ceremony brisk.
- Encourage short acceptance speeches.
- Take a winner photo after each award or one group photo at the end.

### After Awards

- Leave photo props out.
- Keep signs up.
- Let people trade prizes if they want.

## Keep It Public-Safe

- Do not include private logistics, access details, private contact information, or guest contact info in printable/public materials.
- If any practical logistics need to be shared, send them directly to guests instead of adding them to this repo.
