# Wrong-Event Activity Ideas

Public-safe activity concepts for Kate's "dress for the wrong event" birthday. These are built for roughly 14-25 guests, keep participation optional, and avoid drinking-game mechanics.

## 1. St. Patrick's Day Field Day Was Booked for the Wrong Party

### Premise

A St. Patrick's Day charity carnival has accidentally been scheduled over Kate's birthday, and the only way to resolve the calendar conflict is a very serious bean bag toss tournament.

### Why it fits the theme

It is visibly the wrong holiday, the wrong season, and the wrong style of party. The existing St. Patrick's Day bean bag toss banner makes it feel like a party rental arrived from a totally different event.

### Setup/materials

- St. Patrick's Day bean bag toss banner
- Bean bags or other soft tossables
- Painter's tape for three throw lines: "Kids' Birthday," "Corporate Offsite," and "Olympic Qualifier"
- Tickets, stickers, or stamps for scoring
- Small prizes such as a ribbon, candy, medal, tiny trophy, or green beads
- Optional sign: "Annual Shamrock Invitational, Somehow"

### How to play

1. Each guest announces the event they thought they were attending.
2. They get three throws at the banner.
3. Before each throw, the host gives a wrong-event prompt:
   - "Throw like you are late to a wedding."
   - "Throw like this is a board meeting icebreaker."
   - "Throw like your team is down by one in the finals."
   - "Throw like you just realized this is not a baby shower."
4. Guests may choose the easy, medium, or dramatic throw line.
5. For 20+ guests, run it in teams by costume category: holidays, ceremonies, corporate events, sports, fantasy, etc.

### Timing

15-20 minutes as a hosted game, or all night as a drop-in station. Keep it moving by limiting each guest or team to three throws.

### Host script

> Terrible news: the St. Patrick's Day fundraiser has been double-booked with several weddings, launches, recitals, court dates, and Kate's birthday. The only legal way to resolve this is bean bags. Please throw with the dignity of the event you meant to attend.

### Scoring/prizes

- 1 ticket for playing
- 1 ticket per successful toss
- 1 bonus ticket for the funniest wrong-event throwing style
- 3 tickets for the highest score
- 2 tickets for runner-up
- Prize title: "Most Athletic Miscommunication"

### Non-drinking variants

Keep it as a field day/carnival game, not a pub game. If guests want a "toast," make it a two-sentence cheer for Kate, a sparkling water salute, or a team chant before throwing.

## 2. Baldur's Gate Launch Party Press Junket

### Premise

The zany Baldur's Gate launch party photo backdrop becomes the official press wall for a game launch nobody dressed for correctly. Guests are treated as quest companions from other events who must pitch why their look belongs in the campaign.

### Why it fits the theme

A launch party is already a specific wrong event, and the fantasy backdrop gives everyone a reason to reinterpret their costume as lore. Guests who know the game can go deep; guests who do not can still play it as a dramatic red-carpet interview.

### Setup/materials

- Baldur's Gate launch party photo backdrop
- Phone or camera
- Prop microphone, clipboard, or "press badge"
- Index cards labeled "Origin Story," "Party Role," and "Critical Failure"
- Optional d20, cardboard sword, crown, goblet, or fake quest scroll
- Tickets, stickers, or stamps

### How to play

1. Guests visit the backdrop solo, in pairs, or in small groups.
2. They pose for one serious launch-party photo and one "I am at the wrong event" photo.
3. The host asks three press questions:
   - "What event were you supposed to be attending?"
   - "What class, job, or party role does your outfit represent?"
   - "What quest do you bring to Kate's birthday campaign?"
4. Each guest draws or rolls one challenge:
   - Critical success: recruit another guest into your party photo.
   - Critical failure: explain your outfit as a tragic backstory.
   - Side quest: pose as a wedding party, conference panel, sports team, or family holiday card.
5. The host gives tickets for completed photos and strong answers.

### Timing

20-25 minutes as a featured red-carpet segment, or all night as a photo station. If the group is shy, start with pairs instead of solo interviews.

### Host script

> Welcome, esteemed members of the press, to the Baldur's Gate launch party that has somehow collided with every other event on the calendar. Please state your name, your wrong event, and the questionable quest you bring to Kate's campaign.

### Scoring/prizes

- 1 ticket for taking a photo
- 1 ticket for answering in character
- 1 bonus ticket for recruiting another guest into a group shot
- Award ideas: "Best Origin Story," "Best Party Composition," "Most Confusing DLC," and "Most Likely to Be an NPC at the Wrong Event"
- Prize: first pick from a tiny prize pile or the right to name the next group photo pose

### Non-drinking variants

Use "mana" as stickers, tickets, candy, or sparkling water. Any dares should be performance prompts, not drink prompts: deliver a prophecy, give a fake review quote, bless another guest's outfit, or announce a side quest.

## 3. Wrong Event Permit Hearing

### Premise

An emergency permit office must decide which wrong event is legally allowed to take over Kate's birthday for five minutes. Guests petition the room with speeches, objections, endorsements, and fake bureaucratic outrage.

### Why it fits the theme

Every costume becomes a competing event with paperwork. It rewards the best backstories, gives quieter guests a team option, and turns the whole party into a civic disaster without requiring a drinking game.

### Setup/materials

- A chair or marked spot as the witness stand
- Spoon, toy hammer, or other fake gavel
- Timer
- Ballots, tickets, stickers, or stamps
- Index cards with prompts such as "State your event," "Make one demand," and "Compliment Kate"
- Prize envelopes or certificates labeled "Approved Event," "Denied With Flair," and "Most Compelling Misunderstanding"
- Optional: use the Baldur's Gate backdrop as the official press area for winners, and place the St. Patrick's Day banner nearby as "Exhibit A" proving the calendar is beyond repair

### How to play

1. Guests play individually or form small "event blocs" by costume vibe.
2. Each person or bloc gets two minutes to prepare a 30-45 second petition.
3. Each petition must include:
   - The event they thought this was
   - One reason their event deserves to take over
   - One compliment, blessing, or ceremonial declaration for Kate
4. Audience members may use one objection or endorsement per round.
5. After all petitions, the group votes on which event receives a temporary permit.

### Timing

25-30 minutes for 14-15 guests if people team up; 15 minutes if run as a lightning round. Cap speeches hard so the joke stays fast.

### Host script

> This emergency session of the Wrong Event Permit Office is now in order. We have received too many calendar invitations and only one birthday. Petitioners, please explain why your wedding, conference, quest, tailgate, recital, holiday, or other administrative nightmare should briefly replace reality.

### Scoring/prizes

- 1 ticket for making or joining a petition
- 1 ticket for a clever objection or endorsement
- 3 tickets for the winning permitted event
- 2 tickets for the best rejected petition
- 1 ticket for the best use of costume evidence
- Prize titles: "Temporary Custody of the Calendar," "Best Legal Fiction," and "Most Official Nonsense"

### Non-drinking variants

Replace toasts with compliments, proclamations, or dramatic blessings. "Penalties" should be silly tasks like wearing a ribbon, holding the gavel for the next round, or posing for a press photo.
