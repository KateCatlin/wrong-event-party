# Activities and run of show

The goal is aggressive hosting: structured, goofy, and a little chaotic, without making people feel trapped in games all night.

## Core hosting mechanic

Every guest gets a name tag when they arrive:

> The event I thought this was: ______

As people arrive, ask them to explain what event they dressed for. This creates instant mingling and gives you material for awards later.

## Suggested run of show

### 0:00-0:30 - Arrival and wrong-event registration

- Give everyone a name tag.
- Take a photo of each look.
- Ask each guest: "What event are you here for?"
- Optional: Have guests write their event on a card and drop it in a bowl.

### 0:30-0:40 - Opening ceremony

- Gather everyone.
- Do a fast roll call where each person announces their wrong event.
- Explain that there will be holiday/event games from dates that are absolutely not 8/1.

### 0:40-0:55 - Easter egg hunt in August

- Hide plastic eggs with candy, tiny prizes, or dares/prompts.
- Add a few "golden eggs" with prize tickets.
- Make some eggs contain weird holiday prompts like "Give a New Year's toast" or "Explain your costume like it is a legal deposition."

### 0:55-1:10 - Valentine's speed compliments

- Pair people up for 60-second rounds.
- Each person gives the other person a dramatic compliment about their wrong-event look.
- At the end, everyone writes one anonymous "valentine" award nomination.

### 1:10-1:25 - Halloween costume courtroom

- Pick 4-6 people or let volunteers go.
- Each person has 30 seconds to defend why their outfit is the most wrong for this party.
- The crowd cheers to vote.

### 1:25-1:40 - New Year's countdown at the wrong time

- Pick an arbitrary time.
- Hand out noisemakers or party hats.
- Do a full 10-second countdown.
- Make everyone toast to something absurd, like "the fiscal year," "the Mariners World Series win," or "Prom 2026."

### 1:40-2:00 - White Elephant in summer

- Use 10-15 tiny wrapped gifts, or let guests opt in with a silly item.
- Keep it fast: one steal max per turn.
- Gifts can be intentionally odd: tiny trophy, holiday ornament, novelty socks, fake medal, toy, candy.

### 2:00+ - Awards ceremony

- Count votes or just host-decide.
- Give out cheap prizes, ribbons, sashes, or trophies.
- Suggested awards:
  - Best Wrong Event
  - Most Committed to the Bit
  - Most Confusing
  - I Wish I Were at That Event Too
  - Best Explanation
  - Best Group Lore

## More game ideas

### Corporate retreat golf tournament

- Put a cup on the floor as a mini golf hole.
- Use a broom, wrapping paper tube, or toy club.
- Everyone gets one putt.
- Winner gets a "Q3 Leadership Excellence" award.

### Prom 2026 photo nominations

- Make a ballot with prom-style superlatives:
  - Most Likely to Be at the Wrong Party
  - Best Dressed for a Completely Different Room
  - Most Formal for No Reason
  - Most Likely to Leave for a Better Event

### Thanksgiving gratitude circle, but wrong

- Each person says what they are grateful for at the event they thought this was.
- Example: "I am grateful the scuba meetup had cake."

### St Patrick's Day coin hunt

- Hide plastic coins.
- Whoever finds the most gets a prize.
- Bonus if someone dressed in green is required to bless the hunt.

### Birthday party game collision

- Play a classic birthday game, but require everyone to stay in character for their wrong event.
- Options: musical chairs, pin the tail, charades, hot potato.

### Murder mystery micro-round

- Give 5 people quick roles.
- One person is secretly "the person who came to the correct party."
- Everyone asks questions for 5 minutes, then guesses the culprit.

## Recommended final lineup

For a party that feels highly hosted but not over-planned, pick 4-5:

1. Wrong-event registration
2. Opening ceremony
3. Easter egg hunt in August
4. Halloween costume courtroom
5. New Year's countdown at the wrong time
6. Awards ceremony
