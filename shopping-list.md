# Shopping list

Source-of-truth buy list for Kate's 8/1 **dress for the wrong event** birthday party. Buy for **15 confirmed guests** with modular add-ons for **up to 25**. Keep the plan food-forward, activity-forward, and non-alcohol-centered.

## Scaling rule of thumb

For every 5 guests above 15, add:

- 2 large pizzas
- 1 large salad kit or salad tray increment
- 1 large chips/crackers/pita item
- 1 dip, salsa, hummus, or spread
- 1 extra veggie or fruit tray increment
- 12-18 non-alcoholic cans or 1 extra gallon pitcher drink
- 10 lb ice
- 15 cups, 10 plates, 25 napkins, and 10 utensil sets
- 1-2 extra prizes if adding award categories

## Food and delivery plan

Default plan: **pizza + salad + snack table + birthday dessert**. Order pizza/salad 24-48 hours ahead if possible and schedule delivery about 30-45 minutes before food should be eaten. Buy shelf-stable snacks 2-5 days ahead; buy produce and dessert the day before or morning of.

| Item | Buy/order for 15 | Scale for 20-25 |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| Large pizzas | 8 total: 3 cheese, 3 pepperoni/meat, 2 vegetarian | 10 total: 4 cheese, 4 pepperoni/meat, 2 vegetarian |
| Optional gluten-free pizza | 1 if useful for guest list | 1-2 if useful for guest list |
| Large catering salads | 2, or 4 bagged salad kits | 2 larger trays, or 5 bagged salad kits |
| Chips/crackers/pita | 4-5 large bags/boxes | 6-7 large bags/boxes |
| Dips/salsa/hummus | 3 tubs/jars | 5 tubs/jars |
| Veggie trays | 2 trays | 2-3 trays |
| Fruit trays | 1-2 trays | 2 trays |
| Dessert | 25 servings | 30 servings if the list grows |
| Candy for prize/activity bowls | 2-3 lb | 4-5 lb |

Avoid anything that requires cooking during the party, complicated plating, or alcohol/drinking-game mechanics.

## Non-alcoholic drink station

Host-supplied drinks should comfortably cover guests who do not bring anything. Alcohol is not a core buy.

| Item | Buy for 15 | Scale for 20-25 |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| Sparkling water/seltzer | 2 cases / 24-36 cans | 3 cases / 36-48 cans |
| Still water | 1 case or a refillable water station | 1-2 cases or refillable water station |
| Fun NA option: lemonade, iced tea, soda, or punch | 2 gallons | 3 gallons |
| Ice | 30-40 lb | 50-60 lb |
| Cups | 75 | 125 |
| Drink labels | 1 roll painter's tape or label stickers | same |
| Markers for cups/drinks | 2-3 Sharpies | 3-4 Sharpies |

Optional only if guests are likely to bring spirits: a small amount of soda water, ginger beer, cola, or citrus. Do not let mixers crowd out the NA basics.

## Core activity supplies

- Name tags: 25-30, labeled `I'm here for the __________ event`
- Thick markers: 8-12
- Sign: `Registration / Explain Yourself`
- Bowl or container for raffle tickets
- Bell, whistle, spoon-and-glass, or other attention-getter
- Timer or phone alarm

Raffle tickets come primarily from the Easter egg hunt.

### Easter egg hunt with raffle tickets

- Plastic eggs: 40-60 for 15 guests; 60-80 for 25
- Raffle tickets: 100-150 for 15 guests; 150-225 for 25
- 5-8 special eggs with extra tickets or prize slips
- Optional candy, stickers, or tiny treats
- One raffle bowl or ticket container

### St. Patrick's Day bean bag toss through the banner

Use the existing **St. Patrick's Day bean bag toss banner**; do not buy a replacement unless it is missing or damaged.

- Bean bags or other soft tossables: 6-8
- Painter's tape for throw lines: `Kids' Birthday`, `Corporate Offsite`, `Olympic Qualifier`
- Score sheet and pen
- Small prize: ribbon, medal, tiny trophy, candy, green beads, or shamrock item
- Optional sign: `Annual Shamrock Invitational, Somehow`

### Walgreens photo backdrop

Order the selected **8x10 ft Walgreens photo backdrop**. Proof the final file at full size, use the highest-resolution export available, and choose matte fabric or matte vinyl to reduce glare.

- 8x10 ft printed Walgreens backdrop
- Backdrop hanger/stand/crossbar sized for 8x10 ft
- Clamps, grommets, pole pocket, or backdrop clips
- Weights/sandbags for the stand
- Steamer or wrinkle-release plan

Plan for **8-12 awards** so the ceremony feels generous but quick.

- 8-12 award ribbons, medals, trophies, crowns, or sashes
- 8-12 prize items: Ring Pops, candy bars, sticker sheets, novelty sunglasses, temporary tattoos, tiny sparkling cider, bubbles, funny mug, socks, candle, thrifted trophy, or reusable tote
- 1 intentionally absurd grand prize trophy/crown/plaque
- Cardstock for certificates and prize labels
- Gift bags or tissue paper: 8-12

## Conditional Christmas carolers items

Default recommendation: **skip the $200 caroling group** unless Kate specifically wants a polished 10-15 minute live-performance bit and the budget is comfortable.

If skipping paid carolers, buy/prepare a cheaper wrong-Christmas moment:

- 10-minute Christmas playlist fake-out
- 15-25 printed DIY carol cards or one short rewritten chorus
- 2-3 Santa hats, tinsel/garland, ornaments, or holiday props for a tiny misplaced Christmas corner
- One sign: `Holiday Program Clearly Arrived at the Wrong Event`

If hiring the carolers anyway:

- $200 performance fee, plus optional tip if desired
- Confirm 10-15 minute set, 2-4 songs max, full Christmas attire, and comfort with the out-of-season joke
- Prep one host intro card: `They are dressed for the wrong event too.`
- Have the next participatory moment ready immediately after: bean bag toss, permit hearing, photo prompt, or awards voting
- Water or NA drinks for performers
