# Food Plan

Public-safe planning notes for a 15-25 person "dress for the wrong event" birthday party. Keep food simple, easy to replenish, and friendly to guests who may be standing or mingling.

## Recommended default plan

**Default: pizza + salad + snack table.** It is the simplest delivery option, scales well if more people are invited, and does not require much setup.

For **15-25 guests**, supply:

- **Pizza:** 8-10 large pizzas total.
  - 3-4 cheese
  - 3-4 pepperoni or other popular meat option
  - 2 vegetarian
  - Optional: 1 gluten-free pizza if useful for the guest list
- **Salad:** 2 large catering salads or 4-5 bagged salad kits.
- **Snack table:** 4-5 chips/crackers/pita items, 3 dips, 2 veggie trays, and 1-2 fruit trays.
- **Dessert:** cupcakes, cookies, or sheet cake sized for 25 servings.
- **Non-alcoholic drinks:** sparkling water, still water, and one fun option like lemonade, iced tea, or soda.

**Ordering timing:** Order pizza and salads 24-48 hours ahead if possible. Schedule delivery for about 30-45 minutes before guests are expected to eat. Buy shelf-stable snacks 2-5 days ahead and produce/dessert the day before or morning of.

## Other simple approaches

### Option 1: Taco or burrito bar delivery

Good if the host wants something more meal-like without cooking.

For **15-25 guests**, order:

- **Main filling:** 6-8 pounds total across chicken, carnitas/beef, and beans or vegetables.
- **Tortillas or bowls:** 30-40 tortillas, or enough bowl bases for 25 servings.
- **Sides:** 2 trays rice, 2 trays beans, chips, salsa, guacamole, and sour cream.
- **Toppings:** lettuce, cheese, onions/cilantro, pico de gallo, and hot sauce.
- **Diet-friendly coverage:** make sure beans/vegetables are enough for vegetarians and keep at least one mild salsa.

**Ordering timing:** Place a catering order 2-4 days ahead. Confirm the day before. Schedule delivery 30 minutes before food time.

### Option 2: Grocery-hosted snack dinner

Best if delivery is unreliable or the host wants maximum flexibility.

For **15-25 guests**, buy:

- **Savory:** 3-4 dips, 4-5 bags chips/crackers/pita, 2 large veggie trays, 2 fruit trays.
- **Protein/filling:** 2-3 sandwich or wrap platters, or 2 rotisserie chickens plus rolls and condiments.
- **Easy extras:** cheese tray, hummus, olives/pickles, popcorn, and pretzels.
- **Dessert:** 25-30 cookies, cupcakes, brownies, or mini desserts.

**Shopping timing:** Buy shelf-stable items 2-5 days ahead. Buy platters, fruit, veggies, and dessert the day before or morning of. Keep backup chips/crackers unopened so they can be saved if unused.

## What to avoid

- Anything that requires cooking during the party.
- Foods that need individual plating, carving, or complicated serving instructions.
- Very messy foods that stain costumes or require lots of utensils.
- Too many spicy-only choices; include mild and vegetarian options.
- Unlabeled common allergens, especially nuts and shellfish.
- Making alcohol or drinking games part of the food plan.

## Drink guidance

Keep drinks simple and non-alcohol-forward. For **15-25 guests**, plan on:

- 2-3 cases sparkling water or seltzer
- 1 case still water, or a refillable water station with cups
- 2-3 pitchers or large bottles of lemonade, iced tea, soda, or another fun non-alcoholic drink
- Ice, cups, napkins, and a marker or labels for cups

Guests can still bring drinks if they want, but the host-supplied basics should cover anyone who does not.
